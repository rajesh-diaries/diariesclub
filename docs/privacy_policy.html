2. The data we collect

We try hard to collect as little as possible. Here is everything.

2.1 From parents and guardians (the account holder)

Required to use the app: - Phone number (Indian +91 only) — for signing in via OTP and for customer-support look-ups. - Family name — a display name you choose for your family, used for greetings inside the app and for admin support.

Automatically collected as you use the app: - Wallet balance and transaction history — top-ups, debits, refunds, coupon redemptions. Card numbers never touch our servers; Razorpay handles all card storage. - Notification device token — registered with Firebase Cloud Messaging (Android) or Apple Push Notification service (iOS) so we can deliver push notifications. We only register this after you grant push permission, and we clear it on sign-out. - Notification preferences — eight on/off toggles you control inside Profile. - Marketing consent — explicit opt-in, defaults OFF for every new account. - Basic technical telemetry — operating system version, app version, and device model, used solely to diagnose problems and improve app stability. Data stays within our Supabase backend; no third-party crash-reporting SDK is used.

2.2 From children (managed by you, the parent)

You set up a profile for each of your children. We collect:

Required: - The child's first name — used for personalised greetings and progress dashboards. - Date of birth (within the last 14 years) — used to send a personalised birthday greeting on the day, to power the birthday-booking countdown, and to deliver age-appropriate content. - Favourite hero character (Rafi, Ellie, Gerry, or Zena) — drives the Adventure tab visuals.

Optional, clearly labelled as optional: - Delivery address — only if you want us to mail physical prizes; can be skipped or cleared at any time.

We never collect photographs. Not of your child, not of birthday parties, not as staff-curated keepsakes. There is no photo upload anywhere in the app. All child avatars are the first letter of the name on a coloured circle. Play Diaries staff also do not photograph children at the venue for marketing or promotional use. This is a deliberate choice — group photos of children would include guests whose parents are not on our platform, and we never want to process children's images at all.

Automatically computed from gameplay: - XP per trait, level, stage, hero card collection, session history, streak milestones. - Reflection entries — notes you optionally write about your child's play session, used to track their developmental growth over time. Visible only to your family account. - Birthday booking interest signal — auto-derived from your in-app behaviour, used only to decide whether to show birthday-party suggestions on your home screen.

2.3 Transactional data

  • Play sessions — venue check-ins, durations, the child who played, amounts paid.
  • Food and FIT orders — items, prices, when ordered, when delivered.
  • Workshop registrations.
  • Birthday enquiries — preferred date, package interest, guest-count estimate, and any special requests you choose to type. The special-request field may include allergy or dietary information, which you provide voluntarily so the venue team can prepare. Note: birthday parties are not paid for through the app — we only capture the enquiry, then our staff follows up via WhatsApp.
  • Push notification dispatch records — the title and body of each push we send you (which may include your child's first name), plus a delivery status. Auto-purged after 7 days — your in-app inbox shows only the past week.
  • Audit log — every privileged server-side action recorded with actor, timestamp, and the values changed. Used to investigate fraud and to demonstrate accountability under DPDP §8.

2.4 What we explicitly do NOT collect

  • Email addresses — we don't ask, we don't store. All communication is via in-app push, SMS for OTP, and phone / WhatsApp for support.
  • Photographs of any kind — see §2.2.
  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA on iOS, GAID on Android) — we don't access, read, or transmit them. The App Tracking Transparency prompt does not appear on iOS because we don't track you.
  • Government IDs — no Aadhaar, no PAN.
  • Precise geolocation — we do not request GPS permission.
  • Browsing history outside the app, contacts, calendars, voice recordings, biometrics, or camera-roll access.

We do not sell or rent any of your data to anyone, for any purpose. Data is shared only with the operational service providers listed in §3 below, and only to the extent strictly necessary to run the app.

5. Children's data — special protections (DPDP §9)

DPDP Act 2023 has strict rules about processing children's data. Here is how we comply:

  • You are the consenting adult. Children themselves do not hold accounts. Everything in the app is managed by your parent/guardian account.
  • Verifiable parental consent. At signup, you tick a box confirming "I am 18+ and a parent or guardian. I agree to Privacy Policy and Terms," and then verify with a phone OTP. The OTP, on a phone number you own, is your verifiable consent.
  • No advertising to children, ever. We have no ads. Marketing notifications default OFF and require your explicit opt-in.
  • No behavioural surveillance. We track in-app gameplay — XP, levels, streaks — only to power the Adventure tab. This data is never used for advertising, never shared with third parties, and never linked to any cross-app identifier. It is gameplay state, not surveillance.
  • You can edit or remove a child's profile at any time from Profile → Edit Child.
  • Age range is 0–14 years per child profile, enforced both in the app and on the server.
  • Grievance Officer for children's data is the same as the company's grievance officer (§1).

7. Your rights

Under DPDP Act 2023 and the App Store / Play Store policies you can:

7.1 Access your data

Open the Profile tab. Everything we hold about you and your family is visible there — family details, children, wallet history, past sessions, orders, workshops, birthday enquiries.

7.2 Correct your data

Profile lets you edit your family name, each child's name, DOB, favourite hero, and delivery address. Optional fields can be cleared back to empty.

7.3 Delete your account

Profile → Delete account (bottom of the screen). You type the literal word DELETE to confirm. The deletion is instant and a single SQL transaction:

  • Your family name is replaced with "Deleted User."
  • Each child's name is replaced with "Deleted Child."
  • Your phone number is overwritten with an unguessable placeholder so the row no longer maps to a real Indian mobile.
  • FCM device token and marketing consent are cleared.
  • Every push notification we ever sent you is deleted outright.

What is retained — and why. Wallet transactions, sessions, orders, and the audit log are retained in anonymised form — they are linked only to your now-anonymised account UUID, with no name or phone. We have to keep these for Indian tax and GST audits (7-year statutory minimum) and for fraud / chargeback investigation. Razorpay separately retains payment records on their side for 7 years under their RBI obligations. Once the statutory windows expire, these records are physically purged.

There is no "undo." Phone, name, and FCM token are physically overwritten in the database, not soft-deleted. If you sign back up with the same phone, you start as a brand-new account with no history.

The deletion flow is identical on iOS, Android, and the web — Apple and Google both require this in-app, and we do not make you email anyone to delete.

7.4 Withdraw your consent

You can withdraw consent for any purpose you previously consented to, as easily as you gave it: - Marketing notifications → Profile → Notifications → toggle off "Marketing" - Optional fields (delivery address) → Profile → Edit Child → clear the field - Push permission → revoke in iOS / Android settings - All consent at once → delete your account (above)

Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing that already happened, but it stops further processing for that purpose from the moment you withdraw.

7.5 Nominate someone (DPDP §14)

DPDP §14 lets you nominate another person to exercise your rights on your behalf in the event of death or incapacity. We do not yet support in-app nomination — please write to the Grievance Officer if you wish to make a nomination, and we will accept it in writing.

8. Notification preferences

Eight categories of notifications, controllable individually from Profile → Notifications. Seven of them default ON because they are operational (e.g. "your session has started," "your order is ready"). Only one — marketing — defaults OFF. Marketing notifications require your explicit opt-in.

9. How we keep your data safe

  • All data in transit is encrypted with TLS 1.2 or higher.
  • The database is encrypted at rest (AES-256) by Supabase.
  • Row-Level Security is enforced on every customer-touched table — one family's data cannot be queried by another family.
  • Card numbers never touch our servers; Razorpay handles tokenisation.
  • OTP codes are hashed (SHA-256) at rest; never stored in plaintext.
  • Razorpay webhooks are signed (HMAC-SHA256) and signature-verified before any wallet credit happens.
  • Every privileged server-side action is recorded in an audit log.
  • Encrypted backups with 7-day point-in-time recovery, all in Mumbai. No off-region copy exists.

9.1 If we ever have a data breach

We do everything reasonable to prevent unauthorised access. If despite all our safeguards a breach ever occurs, we will:

  1. Notify you — via in-app message and SMS — within the timelines mandated by DPDP Act 2023, telling you exactly what data was affected and what to do about it.
  2. Notify the Data Protection Board of India within the legally mandated window.
  3. Investigate, remediate, and publish a post-incident summary in the app's Help section.

We will never attempt to silently absorb a breach.

9.2 Who at Diaries Club can see your data

A small team within Planovative Diaries LLP has access to the Diaries Club Admin Console — the Designated Partners and a strictly limited number of trained venue staff and customer-support personnel. They can see your wallet history, your sessions, your orders, and the basic details of the children in your family — strictly for customer support, fraud investigation, and operational duties.

Every staff action is recorded in the audit log. You may, at any time, ask the Grievance Officer for a list of admin accesses that touched your specific account, and we will return it within 7 working days.

9.3 Significant Data Fiduciary self-assessment (DPDP §10)

Based on our current customer base and the volume / sensitivity of data we process, Planovative Diaries LLP is not currently classified as a Significant Data Fiduciary under DPDP Act 2023. We re-evaluate this annually and will publicly notify users if our classification ever changes.

11. Government and law-enforcement requests

We respect lawful process while protecting our customers' privacy.

  • We only respond to requests on official letterhead that cite a specific Indian statutory provision (e.g. Section 91 CrPC, IT Act §69, DPDP §17). Informal phone calls or messages are politely declined and asked to be re-issued through proper channels.
  • We push back on overbroad requests and require the requester to specify identified users and the minimum data needed.
  • We notify the affected user wherever legally permitted (some orders carry a gag).
  • We do not maintain a "law-enforcement portal" and we do not pre-emptively volunteer data to anyone.

If you ever want to know whether a request involving your data has been made, ask the Grievance Officer (§1).
